# Fresh-reader review loop

Use this checklist when a complete draft exists and you want a final structure-and-content pass before handing it back.

## Checklist

- Can a new reader understand the purpose from the title and opening paragraph?
- Is the audience obvious from the framing and assumed knowledge level?
- Does the section structure match the document type?
- Are scope, decisions, next steps, and follow-up links easy to find?
- Are any sections too thin to be useful or too long for their importance?
- Are important assumptions, dates, versions, and references explicit?

## Hidden-assumption checks

- Does the draft rely on context from a meeting, chat, or prior doc that is not linked?
- Are domain terms or acronyms defined when a fresh reader would need them?
- Would someone revisiting the document later still know what action to take?

## Handoff rule

- Stay in `draft` mode when structure or content still needs work.
- Switch to `readability-audit` only after the structure is settled and the remaining issue is prose quality.
